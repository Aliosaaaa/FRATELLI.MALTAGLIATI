Guardians of the Land

The forest is dying.
We chose to save it.

Centuries-old chestnut groves bent by disease, neglect and the total abandonment of the land. Giacomo and Giulio Maltagliati decided to roll up their sleeves.

Save the forest with us Discover our methodarrow_forward

A heritage vanishing in silence.

Forest abandonment is not only an aesthetic loss. It is an open wound in the heart of our biodiversity. Without care, the chestnut grove succumbs to parasites, the soil weakens and millennia of rural culture are erased by the dust of time.

«Every chestnut tree that falls is a chapter of our story we will never read again.»— Nonno Nanne

Giacomo & Giulio

Fratelli Maltagliati

Those who chose to return.

Giacomo and Giulio are not just farmers. They are survivors of a frantic world they chose to trade for the slowness of the seasonal cycles.

Armed with brushcutters, patience and a visceral love for their land, they began to wrestle the family's historic chestnut groves from the suffocating embrace of brambles. Every chestnut that today falls onto cleared ground is a victory against oblivion.

The Brothers

Giacomo and Giulio. The hands.

Raised among the metati and the woods of the Svizzera Pesciatina, the Maltagliati brothers inherited from their grandfathers and fathers the knowledge of a land that few still know how to care for today. They chose to come back. To take up the axe, the shovel, the millstone again.

This is not a romantic venture: it is an act of resistance against abandonment. Every chestnut tree pruned, every metre of cleared forest, is a small victory of the land over oblivion.
